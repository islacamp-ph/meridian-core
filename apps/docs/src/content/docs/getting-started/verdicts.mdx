---
title: Verdicts
description: Understanding CLEAR, WARN, and ABORT
---

MERIDIAN returns one of three verdicts after running the full analysis pipeline.

## CLEAR

The transaction is predicted to succeed with no critical risks. Confidence must meet the configured threshold (default 0.75) for a `CLEAR` verdict.

## WARN

The transaction may succeed but carries advisory risks — TTL warnings, low confidence, staleness, or elevated blast radius. Review the `warnings` array and `fix_sequence` before submitting.

## ABORT

A critical failure is predicted — auth failure, archived entry, simulation error, or catastrophic blast radius. Do not submit without remediation.

## Confidence

Confidence (0–1) reflects simulation quality, manifest coverage, and evidence strength. The `meta.confidence_bucket` field classifies it as `LOW`, `MEDIUM`, or `HIGH`.

## Fix sequences

On `WARN` and `ABORT` verdicts, MERIDIAN returns a numbered `fix_sequence` with remediation steps, estimated cost in stroops, and estimated time.
